Jake Thornock is the John W. and Nancy S. Hardy Chaired Professor of Accounting at the Marriott School of Business, Brigham Young University, where he also serves as Associate Director of the Executive MBA program.

His research examines how information intermediaries, investor attention, and corporate disclosure shape capital markets, along with the causes and consequences of corporate tax avoidance. His work has appeared in the Journal of Finance, the Journal of Financial Economics, the Journal of Accounting Research, the Journal of Accounting and Economics, The Accounting Review, and Management Science, among others.

Before joining BYU, he was a tenured member of the accounting faculty at the University of Washington's Foster School of Business. He earned his PhD in Accounting from the University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill and his MAcc and BS from Brigham Young University.

About

Jake Thornock studies information intermediaries, investor attention, disclosure, and corporate taxation. His research asks how information flows to and through capital markets — via the internet, social media, financial statement users, and short sellers — and how firms and investors respond. A second stream examines the determinants and real effects of corporate tax avoidance.
